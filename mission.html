Leveraging the Ecosystem

One Platform. Every Device.

The Motorola Solutions ecosystem collapses information silos, unifying response agencies through a resilient, closed-loop cloud-native platform. Because Motorola already supplies the hardware, CC Aware connects it all — no third-party middleware required.

🚁

Drone as First Responder (DFR)

Autonomous drones deploy before ground units and stream live aerial video directly into CC Aware's map, providing immediate situational awareness at the scene.

📷

Avigilon Fixed Video

Avigilon Unity (acquired by Motorola in 2018) provides AI-powered fixed camera analytics. Video feeds and detection events feed natively into CC Aware without integration middleware.

🚗

V700, M500 & SVX

In-car video (V700) and body-worn cameras (M500, SVX) stream live footage directly onto the incident map, giving command center operators a real-time ground-level view.

📻

APX NEXT Radios

APX NEXT LMR radios transmit GPS location via AVL, populating unit positions on the CC Aware map in real time. Radio voice remains operational even if broadband fails.

🚨

Rave Panic Button

Acquired by Motorola in 2021, Rave Panic Button triggers instant CC Aware Rules Engine responses — nearest cameras surface automatically, supervisors are alerted, and a Mission can auto-launch.

🔍

License Plate Recognition (LPR)

LEARN and Vigilant LPR hits appear as map events in CC Aware. Patrol officers and dispatch see plate reads in context with active incidents — critical for pursuit and evacuation management.

Validated by Government

Royal Commission Confirms the Approach

Australia's Royal Commission into Natural Disasters independently validated the exact technology strategy Motorola Solutions has built CC Aware around.

"Robust, mission-critical communications including land mobile radio remains the most reliable technology for public safety agencies."
Royal Commission into Natural Disasters — Final Report, accepted in full by the Australian Government · Nov. 2020
📻

LMR Is the Gold Standard

The Commission found that Land Mobile Radio has proven to be the most resilient, reliable, and secure technology to withstand the impacts of natural disasters — including bushfires — in all countries worldwide. CC Aware is built on this backbone.

🤝

Cross-Jurisdiction Interoperability

As fires cross state lines, the Commission called for enhanced interoperability between agencies and jurisdictions. CC Aware's cloud-based Missions platform links disparate networks — giving agencies a shared operational picture across county and state borders.

🚁

Drones as First Responders

The Commission highlighted drones as having "huge potential in bushfire recovery," acting as the first first responders — creating eyes on the scene before any human resources are deployed. This is exactly what CC Aware's Drone as First Responder (DFR) integration delivers.

🤖

AI + Data Fusion at the Frontline

The report called for voice communications to be augmented with rich data sets leveraging AI and analytics — to build a more complete picture of real-time incident management. CC Aware's Rules Engine and AI Assist are precisely this capability.

📡

Broadband Enhances, LMR Anchors

The Commission endorsed fusing LMR and broadband technologies — not replacing one with the other. CC Aware operates this hybrid model natively: radio remains operational even when broadband fails in high-WUI terrain.

🔮

AI to Predict and Prevent

The Commission's vision: leverage AI and sophisticated analytics to help predict and even prevent bushfires. Motorola's ICEYE SAR satellite integration and CC Aware's weather and fire perimeter overlays are the first step toward exactly this outcome.
